    Step By Step CleaningThe standard carpet cleaning service typically includes the following:

    Pre-Inspection: A professional carpet cleaner will assess the condition of your carpets, noting any heavily soiled or stained areas and determining the best approach for cleaning.
    Pre-Vacuuming: Before the deep cleaning process begins, the carpet may be vacuumed thoroughly to remove loose dirt, dust, and debris from the surface and fibers.
    Pre-Treatment: Stubborn stains and high-traffic areas may receive pre-treatment with specialized cleaning solutions. These solutions help break down dirt and stains, making them easier to remove during the cleaning process.
    Hot Water Extraction (Steam Cleaning): This method is commonly used for deep cleaning carpets. It involves the application of hot water and a cleaning solution under high pressure into the carpet fibers. The solution is then extracted along with the loosened dirt and stains using a powerful vacuum.
    Spot Treatment: Any remaining stubborn stains may be individually treated to ensure they are effectively removed.
    Post-Cleaning Inspection: Once the cleaning process is complete, a final inspection may be conducted to ensure all areas have been thoroughly cleaned and any remaining spots or stains have been addressed.
    Drying: Depending on the cleaning method used, the carpet may require some time to dry completely. Techniques such as using air movers or opening windows to improve ventilation may be employed to speed up the drying process.
    Optional Add-Ons: Some carpet cleaning services may offer additional treatments or services, such as carpet deodorizing, carpet protection application (e.g., Scotchgard), or specialty stain removal for an extra cost. It's important to communicate with your carpet cleaning provider to understand exactly what is included in their standard service and if there are any additional options or packages available to meet your specific needs.
